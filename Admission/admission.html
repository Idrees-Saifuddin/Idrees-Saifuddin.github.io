Our Policies

Registration:
  • All the visitors interested in admission of their wards are requested to fill a visitor's slip available at reception.
  • The P.R.O arranges an initial meeting with principal who provides detailed information about our Institution and curriculum etc. The principal will interview the child and the parents, & then will offer an admission on spot if basic requirements for admission including availability of seats are met. Parents have to collect ADMISSION FORM from the reception.
Regular Classes:

After completion of admission formalities the child can attend the classes.

Exercise books and Stationeries:

Montessories items will be provided by the school in classroom.

Uniform:

There is uniform for Montessori students. Students must come in neat and tidy dresses.

Attendance:

The student must maintain 90% attendance. Absence due to any unavoidable circumstances must be followed by a written application duly signed by the parents in case of prolonged absence due to ill health, a medical certificate should be submitted with leave application. An absence of any five continues working days without prior information may result in drop page of the students from our roll.

Code of Conduct:

All students must observe the school rules within the school premises.

  • Students should be neatly dressed.
  • Boys should have a neat gentlemanly hair cut and girls should have simple hair-do. Girls are not to put on jewellery.
  • Absentees, upon return, must produce a medical certificate or documentary evidence as the case may be along with an application duly endorsed by parents/guardians.
  • Students must qualify all class tests, terminal and annual examination.

Message

High standards and expectations for each student in regard to academic performance, co-curricular participation, and responsible citizenship are the foundation of our school. It is with pride that we hold these high standards and ask each of our students to commit to maintaining the extraordinary record of achievement and contribution that has been the legacy of The Foundation School students.
